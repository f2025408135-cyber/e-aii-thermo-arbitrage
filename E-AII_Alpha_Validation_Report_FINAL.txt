5.1 Objection: "The Efficient Market Hypothesis Eliminates This
Alpha"
"If this signal were real, rational market participants would have already arbitraged it away. The
EMH implies that all publicly available information, including weather data, is already reflected in
GPU compute prices."
Refutation: This objection conflates information availability with information processing. The EMH
assumes that market participants can and do process all available information into price adjustments
instantaneously and at zero cost. This assumption is violated in the GPU compute marketplace for three
structural reasons. First, the relevant information (boundary-layer wind speed, AHF, convective suppression
indices) is published by meteorological agencies in raw telemetry format, not in the processed M_drift
metric that the engine computes. Converting raw atmospheric data into a margin erosion coefficient requires
domain-specific expertise in atmospheric thermodynamics, building physics, and power grid operations, a
knowledge intersection that is extremely rare among GPU compute market participants. Second, the
relevant marketplace (Vast.ai and RENDER token contracts) is populated primarily by individual GPU
owners and small-scale miners who lack the analytical infrastructure to correlate atmospheric conditions
with their operating margins. Third, even if a sophisticated participant possessed both the data and the
model, the latency between atmospheric observation and marketplace response is measured in hours to
days, not milliseconds, because there is no automated pipeline from meteorological data feeds to GPU spot
pricing algorithms. The alpha exists precisely because no market participant currently operates such a
pipeline, and building one requires cross-domain expertise that is not incentivized by any existing market
structure.

5.3 Objection: "Execution Risk Invalidates the Alpha"
"A 43.53% win rate in real-world conditions is barely above a coin flip. The strategy loses on

loss rate is concentrated in small-spread signals (128-165 bps raw spread), where friction effects are
proportionally larger. The large-spread signals (2,217-4,910 bps raw spread) retain near-100% win rates
even under friction, because the slippage of 180 bps is small relative to the 4,910 bps peak spread. Third,

trade size), while the maximum per-trade gain was approximately $1,690 (42.3% of trade size). This
extreme positive skew is the structural signature of a genuine edge, not a coin flip.

7. Forward Path: Predictive Nowcasting MPC
The friction-hardened simulation reveals that execution latency is the single largest profit destruction vector
after Kyle Lambda slippage, responsible for $1,598,675.37 in aggregate spread degradation across 13,593
partially degraded fills. The mean latency of 30 seconds consumes 10% of the 300-second signal window,
and the resulting alpha decay reduces the average degradation factor to 0.89. If the engine could predict the

eliminated, and the degradation factor would approach 1.0 for the majority of trades.
The predictive nowcasting MPC (Model Predictive Control) framework achieves this through a three-step
architecture. Step 1: Feature Engineering extracts 5-minute lag features from the historical telemetry
database (1800-2026, 5-minute intervals), including rolling means and standard deviations of u_wind, AHF,
CAPE, CIN, and derived quantities (stagnation ratio, heat flux density, M_drift trajectory). Step 2:
Nowcasting Model trains a gradient-boosted tree (GBT) ensemble with LSTM residuals on these features
to predict M_drift 30 minutes ahead. The target variable is the future M_drift at T+30min, and the model is
trained to minimize MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) with a target threshold of < 5.5%. Step 3:
MPC State Machine implements a three-state controller: COLD (no predicted stagnation, no
pre-positioning), PRE_FLIGHT_WARMUP (predicted_M_drift > 1.50, begin pre-positioning at 50% target
allocation), and ACTIVE (M_drift > 1.10 confirmed, full allocation). The PRE_FLIGHT_WARMUP state
is triggered when the nowcast predicts M_drift > 1.50 within the next 30 minutes, providing the 30-minute
head start needed to eliminate execution latency as a friction factor.
The projected impact of the nowcasting MPC on friction-hardened performance is substantial. If the latency

execution), the aggregate latency destruction decreases from $1,598,675 to approximately $320,000,
